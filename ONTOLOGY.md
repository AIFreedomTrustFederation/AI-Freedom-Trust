# Federation Ontology

Status: canonical doctrine draft  
Scope: shared vocabulary for AI Freedom Trust Federation repositories, trust seeds, agents, and applications

## Purpose

This ontology defines the core words of the AI Freedom Trust Federation so humans, repositories, manifests, and AI agents use the same language.

The goal is not to freeze meaning forever. The goal is to create a stable shared vocabulary that can be versioned, reviewed, refined, and inherited by every local trust seed.

## Core Terms

### Federation

The voluntary network of sovereign local trusts, repositories, applications, communities, businesses, research projects, and human stewards that share a constitutional pattern, interoperable tools, and consent-based coordination.

The Federation is not merely a centralized platform. It is a pattern that can be locally instantiated.

### Trust

A sovereign stewardship structure for a person, family, business, ministry, cooperative, community, repository, research project, or other mission-bearing entity.

A trust is not merely an account. It is a local expression of the Federation pattern.

### Trust Seed

The minimum local starter pattern required to instantiate a trust.

A trust seed includes identity, values, governance, Tree of Life, economy, AI steward, Living Atlas context, local manifest, and optional Federation Link.

### Holographic Principle

The architectural rule that the whole Federation pattern should be present inside every local trust seed without requiring centralized possession of each trust's private data.

The whole exists in every part as structure, not as surveillance.

### Green Zone

The user's local present layer where identity, location, current tasks, relationships, consent, and observation meet.

In interface terms, the Green Zone is the center of the user's Living Atlas. In governance terms, it is the current decision context. In agentic terms, it is the permitted working memory boundary.

### Living Atlas

The zoomable map of local and global Federation reality.

It may represent Earth, places, people, businesses, repositories, projects, missions, contributions, assets, events, and communities through privacy-preserving local and federated layers.

### Tree of Life

The growth model of a trust.

Seed is origin and consent. Roots are foundations. Trunk is enduring mission. Branches are projects. Leaves are active tasks. Fruit is completed value. New seeds are future trusts, projects, and communities born from completed work.

### Federation Link

The permissioned boundary between a local trust and the wider Federation.

A Federation Link defines what the trust chooses to publish, sync, verify, index, or share.

### AI Steward

An AI assistant operating under trust-defined permissions.

The AI steward supports human agency, documentation, memory, reasoning, drafting, search, and coordination. It is not the owner of the trust and must not override consent.

### Contribution

Any action that creates value for a trust, project, community, repository, public good, or person.

Examples include code, documentation, review, research, design, local repair, mentoring, delivery, teaching, governance, support, and stewardship.

### Mission

A scoped invitation to create value.

A mission may be a task, bounty, local service request, research need, bug fix, documentation request, educational assignment, or community project.

### Fruit

Completed contribution that can nourish others.

Examples include releases, working software, repaired infrastructure, published research, income-generating services, educational materials, public goods, and documented lessons.

### Coherence

The condition where differentiated contributions, evidence, people, documents, repositories, or systems become aligned enough to produce a stable result.

Coherence does not require uniformity. It requires truthful relationship between parts.

### Public Maturity Label

A label describing the evidence status of a claim, project, paper, feature, or deployment.

Examples include conceptual, exploratory, reproducible, awaiting external review, prototype, production-facing, revenue-unverified, audited, or externally validated.

## Design Rule

Every new repository, document, app, manifest, and agent should reuse these terms unless there is a clear reason to introduce a new one.

New terms should be added here before they become operational dependencies.
