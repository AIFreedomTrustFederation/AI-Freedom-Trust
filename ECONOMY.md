# Federation Economy

Status: canonical doctrine draft  
Scope: contribution, marketplaces, local commerce, rewards, assets, and compliance boundaries

## Purpose

The Federation economy exists to help people, trusts, communities, and repositories exchange productive value.

The economy should serve stewardship, repair, education, local resilience, useful software, research, and public goods.

## Core Principle

The Federation should not be centered on empty speculation.

It should be centered on productive assets, useful services, transparent contribution, lawful exchange, and community stewardship.

## Economic Layers

### Contribution Ledger

Records useful work:

- code;
- documentation;
- review;
- research;
- design;
- education;
- local service;
- repair;
- delivery;
- mentoring;
- governance;
- infrastructure support.

### Mission and Bounty Layer

Lets people and organizations post scoped work with clear acceptance criteria and possible rewards.

### Local Commerce Layer

Helps users discover and support local businesses, farms, services, cooperatives, ministries, and community projects.

### Knowledge Marketplace

Supports lawful exchange of educational materials, software components, AI tools, datasets, research summaries, templates, and documentation.

### AI Marketplace

Allows useful AI agents, prompts, workflows, and local assistants to be published, reviewed, improved, licensed, or supported where permitted.

### Community Funding Layer

Communities may use donations, memberships, grants, sponsorships, cooperative funding, bounties, or other lawful mechanisms to support productive projects.

### Asset and Token Layer

Some communities may eventually explore digital assets, tokenized records, memberships, credits, or investment-like instruments.

Any such activity requires jurisdiction-specific legal, tax, and compliance review before public deployment. The repository must not present conceptual asset designs as approved financial products.

## Value Types

The system should distinguish:

- donation;
- purchase;
- service payment;
- membership;
- grant;
- bounty;
- reward;
- revenue share;
- cooperative patronage;
- loan;
- investment;
- regulated security or financial product.

Clear labels protect users and stewards.

## Play-to-Earn Refinement

The Federation should prefer contribute-to-earn.

Useful participation may earn recognition, access, opportunities, grants, bounties, service payments, or community trust. Rewards should be connected to real contribution and clear records.

## Marketplace Integrity

Listings should include:

- seller or steward identity;
- offering description;
- price or reward;
- delivery expectations;
- refund or dispute terms;
- maturity or evidence label;
- compliance notes if needed;
- public or private visibility level.

## Stewardship Boundaries

The economy must not override human dignity, consent, privacy, or local sovereignty.

Economic systems should make value visible without turning people into mere metrics.

## AI Role

AI stewards may help:

- draft listings;
- summarize contribution ledgers;
- explain risks;
- classify value types;
- flag missing compliance review;
- prepare mission acceptance criteria;
- identify local opportunities.

AI must not represent an investment, legal structure, or regulated offering as approved unless qualified review is documented.

## Design Goal

The Federation economy should feel like a living ecosystem: seeds become projects, projects become branches, work becomes fruit, and fruit nourishes new seeds.
