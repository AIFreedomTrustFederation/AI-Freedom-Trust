# Federation Governance

Status: canonical doctrine draft

## Purpose

Governance defines how the Federation, trusts, repositories, agents, and communities make decisions while preserving consent, local sovereignty, transparency, and repair.

## Core Principle

The Federation is not a command hierarchy. It is a voluntary network of sovereign local trusts joined by shared constitutional principles and interoperable tools.

## Governance Layers

### Personal and Trust Governance

Each trust defines its own decision process, privacy boundaries, stewardship roles, and amendment process.

### Repository Governance

Each repository defines maintainers, review rules, maturity labels, contribution requirements, release rules, and evidence boundaries.

### Community Governance

Each community defines membership, missions, local rules, conflict repair, resource stewardship, and public commitments.

### Federation Governance

The Federation defines shared doctrine, interoperability standards, AI stewardship principles, manifest schemas, and constitutional amendments.

## Consent

Consent must be clear before sensitive actions.

Sensitive actions include publishing private data, changing public doctrine, altering governance rules, committing regulated economic claims, exposing location, or granting new AI permissions.

## Evidence Boundaries

Governance must preserve the distinction between:

- doctrine;
- evidence;
- research;
- prototype;
- production;
- legal claim;
- financial claim;
- empirical claim.

Claims should not be strengthened beyond their support.

## Decision Records

Important decisions should be recorded in Markdown, Git history, issues, proposals, or other inspectable records.

A good decision record explains what changed, why it changed, who approved it, what risks remain, and what must be reviewed later.

## Repair

The preferred governance path is repair before removal when safe and appropriate.

Repair may include clarification, apology, correction, amendment, review, mediation, boundary-setting, or professional help when needed.

## AI Governance

AI agents may assist governance, but they do not replace consent.

Agents should summarize evidence, draft options, identify risks, preserve uncertainty, and request approval before sensitive actions.

## Design Goal

Governance should feel like stewardship: clear enough to protect people, flexible enough to support local life, and transparent enough for trust to grow.
