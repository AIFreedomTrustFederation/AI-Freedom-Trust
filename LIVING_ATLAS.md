# Living Atlas

Status: canonical doctrine draft  
Scope: Earth-centered, local-first Federation map interface

## Purpose

The Living Atlas is the Federation's map of local and global reality.

It centers each user, trust, repository, project, business, and community in its own frame of reference while showing how that local node belongs to a wider voluntary network.

## Core Principle

Every user is the center of their own interface universe.

This does not mean one user is more important than another. It means the system organizes itself around the user's current context, location, responsibilities, permissions, and relationships.

A map application centers on the user's location. The Living Atlas centers on the user's whole Green Zone.

## Green Zone

The Green Zone is the user's living present.

It may include:

- approximate or precise location, as permitted;
- current trust identity;
- current tasks;
- active projects;
- local community;
- family and trusted circle;
- nearby missions;
- local businesses and services;
- repositories connected to local work;
- AI steward context;
- governance decisions awaiting attention.

## Zoom Model

The Living Atlas should support a continuous zoom path:

1. Planet
2. Continent
3. Country
4. State or region
5. City
6. Neighborhood
7. Trust
8. Project
9. Repository
10. Document
11. Task
12. Contribution record

Every level should preserve the same pattern: identity, relationships, past record, present work, future path, and contribution flow.

## Tree of Life Overlay

Each trust may display a Tree of Life overlay:

- Seed: origin and consent.
- Roots: foundations and relationships.
- Trunk: enduring mission.
- Branches: projects and collaborations.
- Leaves: active work.
- Fruit: completed value.
- New seeds: future trusts, projects, communities, and opportunities.

The Tree of Life should be both visual and operational. It should help users understand what they are building and what needs attention next.

## Privacy Layers

Location and relationship data are sensitive.

The Living Atlas must support privacy layers:

- private local only;
- trusted circle;
- community approximate;
- public project;
- public business;
- event-specific temporary visibility;
- fully public Federation node.

The user or trust should choose what becomes visible.

## Local Missions

The Living Atlas should make local value visible.

Examples:

- repair work;
- food distribution;
- community gardens;
- local commerce;
- educational events;
- veteran support;
- repository bounties;
- research needs;
- mentorship;
- mutual aid;
- business partnerships.

## Repository Layer

Repositories should appear as living nodes, not static links.

A repository node may display:

- mission;
- README summary;
- current branch;
- issues;
- pull requests;
- releases;
- contributors;
- related trusts;
- documentation;
- maturity label;
- AI steward summary.

## Marketplace Layer

Marketplace activity must remain consent-based and legally bounded.

The Living Atlas may show public offerings, missions, services, bounties, memberships, grants, and other opportunities, but regulated activity requires appropriate review before deployment.

## Design Goal

The visitor should feel that the Federation is not an abstract platform. It is a living network of places, people, projects, repositories, and communities that begins where they are.
