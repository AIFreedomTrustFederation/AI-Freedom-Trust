# Tree of Life

Status: canonical doctrine draft

## Purpose

The Tree of Life is the Federation's growth model for people, trusts, repositories, projects, businesses, families, and communities.

It replaces shallow game mechanics with an ecological model of contribution, stewardship, and renewal.

## Pattern

### Seed

The seed is origin, consent, identity, values, and first intention.

### Roots

Roots are the foundations that nourish the trust: family, land, skills, documents, history, relationships, faith, discipline, and local reality.

### Trunk

The trunk is the enduring mission, the stable identity, and the repeated commitments that hold the trust upright.

### Branches

Branches are active projects, repositories, businesses, ministries, research efforts, collaborations, and missions.

### Leaves

Leaves are daily work: tasks, messages, commits, documents, services, meetings, and local actions.

### Fruit

Fruit is completed value: releases, repaired systems, income, food, education, public goods, services delivered, lessons learned, and people helped.

### New Seeds

New seeds are future trusts, projects, communities, repositories, and opportunities born from completed fruit.

## Contribution Model

A user's growth should be measured by contribution, not empty status.

Useful work grows the tree. Stewardship strengthens the forest.

## Interface Model

The Living Atlas may show a user's Tree of Life centered in their Green Zone.

Users should be able to see what is rooted, what is active, what is bearing fruit, and what is ready to seed something new.

## Federation Model

Every trust is a tree. Every community is a forest. The Federation is an ecosystem of forests voluntarily connected through shared principles and interoperable tools.
