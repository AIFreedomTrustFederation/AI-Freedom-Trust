# Mission System

Status: canonical doctrine draft  
Scope: contribution, learning, bounties, quests, and stewardship workflows

## Purpose

The Mission System turns meaningful contribution into visible, trackable, and reviewable work.

The goal is to help people discover useful work, complete it with accountability, and grow their Tree of Life through real contribution.

## Core Principle

People should not have to pay to become valuable. They become valuable by creating value.

The Federation should therefore prefer contribute-to-earn over pay-to-play.

## Mission Types

### Learning Missions

Help a user gain knowledge or skill.

Examples:

- read a doctrine document;
- complete a tutorial;
- learn a tool;
- summarize a paper;
- practice a workflow.

### Repository Missions

Improve software, documentation, research, or infrastructure.

Examples:

- fix a bug;
- write tests;
- update a README;
- review a pull request;
- improve accessibility;
- reproduce a research result.

### Local Missions

Create value in a place.

Examples:

- deliver food;
- repair equipment;
- help a local business;
- plant or maintain a garden;
- support a veteran;
- organize a community event.

### Stewardship Missions

Help people, projects, or communities grow responsibly.

Examples:

- mentor a new contributor;
- resolve a dispute;
- maintain a project;
- document a process;
- verify a claim;
- improve governance.

### Economic Missions

Create productive value through goods, services, grants, bounties, or public goods.

Examples:

- complete paid work;
- deliver a product;
- fulfill a bounty;
- publish an educational resource;
- create a reusable tool;
- support a community project.

## Mission Record

Every mission should define:

- title;
- purpose;
- location or repository context;
- required skills;
- acceptance criteria;
- steward or verifier;
- privacy level;
- reward or recognition, if any;
- completion evidence;
- follow-up seeds.

## Growth Model

Missions grow the Tree of Life:

- learning strengthens roots;
- repeated practice strengthens the trunk;
- projects become branches;
- tasks become leaves;
- completed outcomes become fruit;
- reusable outcomes become new seeds.

## Rewards

Rewards may include recognition, reputation, access to tools, mentorship, grants, bounties, revenue share, service payment, community trust, or future opportunities.

Rewards must be transparent and lawful.

## Stewardship Rule

The Mission System should reward repair, truthfulness, contribution, mentorship, stewardship, and useful work.

## AI Role

AI stewards may help users find missions, break missions into steps, draft deliverables, check acceptance criteria, summarize completion evidence, and propose next missions.

AI must not falsely verify work it cannot inspect.

## Design Goal

The user should feel that every meaningful action grows their tree and strengthens the forest.
